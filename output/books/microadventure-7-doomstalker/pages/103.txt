While LoDar adjusts the dials on the

on your portable computer. It spews out the trip

mission.
You've just slipped the disk into your
pocket when LoDar turns to you. "Give me
your computer, Orion," he asks. It's not really
a request, it's an order. "I presume you had a
built-in trip log," he adds as he passes the
computer through a magnetized circle. You
flinch as the field pulses on and off. You know
that every bit of memory in your banks has just
been reduced to zeros.
"And now, my friends, this is good-bye.
ACT's help has been invaluable, and I only wish

way."
"I wish we could remember you, at least,"
