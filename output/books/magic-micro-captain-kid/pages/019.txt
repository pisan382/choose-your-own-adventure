Chapter 2
Captain Robb shakes his head sadly. “I’d
bet my life that this is the right key. I won
it in a card game — from old Black Beard
himself. I’ve been carrying it around for
twenty years. He took a pirate’s oath that
it would open the chest. I just can’t un­
derstand why it’s not working.”
You can hear the crew growling and
muttering all around you. They don’t seem
too pleased with this development.
“It’s a trick,” Darcy says. “Captain Robb
wants to keep the treasure for himself!
“Stand back,” Bard growls, pushing
Captain Robb aside. “Who needs a key,
anyway?’’ He picks up a heavy wooden
hammer and whacks the trunk. It doesn’t
open, but it makes a groaning noise.
