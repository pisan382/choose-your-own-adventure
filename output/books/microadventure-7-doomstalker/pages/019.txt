which the ACT team eats
sitting on a large boulder overlooking the
spectacular valley below. If you were seeing this
from the comfort of a movie theater, you'd be
impressed with the view. As it is, looking up
makes you dizzy and looking down is even
worse. And your feet feel as though you've
trekked from Nepal to China.
Just then, your solid seat starts to vi-
brate. And all around you rocks begin to slide.
"There's an overhang at the foot of the
boulder. Get down there quick," Bronze yells.
"It's another earthquake!"
The four of you huddle in the big rock's
protection as pieces of earth and stone tumble
past. If you'd stayed up on that narrow ledge,
you would have been goners. You glance over
at Arch and Frieda. They look as pale as the
snow-covered peaks. And you suspect your face
is just as white.
