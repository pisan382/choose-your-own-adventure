Chapter 4
Everybody is cooped up below deck to
get out of the rain. After sitting around
cramped like sardines for hours, nobody
is in a good mood anymore.
Maybe, you think, you can cheer them
up if you issue extra rations. But when you
break into the supplies, your heart sinks.
Half the boxes of biscuits turn out to be
imported bubble bath instead.
The crew is even angrier than you ex­
pected. “We can’t eat that stuff. And we
never take baths,’’ Bard growls. From the
odor of the air below deck, you know he’s
telling the truth.
“You’re the captain. Think of some­
thing we can do,” Pete snaps.
