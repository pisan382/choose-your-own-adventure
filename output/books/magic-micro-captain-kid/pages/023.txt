This time, when Captain Robb inserts
the key, it turns easily and the lock snaps
open. Everyone cheers.
“We’ve been waiting for twenty years
to get into this chest,” Captain Robb ex­
claims. “I can’t wait to get my hands on the
gold and jewels inside. And I want to thank
all the people who made this moment
possible.”
“Hip! Hip! Hooray!” the crew shouts.
Hal steps forward and lifts the heavy lid.
He bends over and peers into the trunk.
First his head disappears, then his shoul­
ders. Whatever he’s found must be way at
the bottom of that trunk. When his head
finally reappears, he doesn’t look as if he’s
found the pot of gold at the end of the
rainbow.
“What do you see, Hal?”
“What is it?”
“What’s in the chest?”
Hal shakes his head sadly. “Sorry, mates.
There’s only this piece of paper.”
