Out of the corner of your eye, you no­
tice dozens of cats with their heads poked
out from behind rocks. Gray cats, black
cats, white cats — every variation of cat
fur you’ve ever imagined. And by the look
of confidence in the mother cat’s eyes, you
realize she knows the odds are in her
favor.
“Mom, please, we were only playing,”
the kitten protests. “Everyone knows that
the Red Queen doesn’t play around.”
“Hush, Leo,” the mother scolds. “You
don’t know what kind of nasty plot the Red
Queen Cerise might be hatching.”
Just then, a loud siren blast splits the air.
At the same time, at the top of the moun­
tain, a revolving warning light starts to
flash. It reminds you of the lights you’ve
seen on top of police cars.
“The Red Queen is coming,” a black cat
shouts.
