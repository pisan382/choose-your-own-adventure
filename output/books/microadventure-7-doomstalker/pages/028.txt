Everyone looks mystified. This is getting
spookier by the moment.
Apparently Bronze agrees. It's obvious
that he'd rather think about more concrete
problems. "Let me go check out the dam-
age," he offers. In a moment he's back, look-
ing like he came in ninth out of 10 in a cross-
country ski race. "Two of our packs were hit."
"What did we lose?" Frieda asks.
"The gifts we were going to give to the
natives, and some of our food. We're going to
be on short rations from now on."
"Oh, no," Frieda exclaims, "not the gifts!
I was counting on them to show the local
tribesmen our goodwill."
She may be worried about goodwill, but
you're more concerned about the food when you
find out that only the dehydrated vegetables and
a few packets of vitamin pills survived. Even
the rest of the marshmallows were charred to
black lumps.
