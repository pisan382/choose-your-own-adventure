"Looks are deceiving. This stuff is a new
fiber that's stronger than steel cable," Arch

with a microprocessor.
"Now I just have to hook up the au to-
matic breeches-buoy and we're all set."
"What's a breeches-buoy?" you ask, not
wanting to sound stupid, but not wanting to risk
your life on some unknown device, either.
"It's like one of those canvas slings they
use to rescue people from burning buildings,"
Arch explains. "Ours is made of a specially
treated silk and weighs only eight ounces. Now,
who wants to go first?" he asks, looping the
buoy's pulley over the thin strands that now
cross the gorge.
